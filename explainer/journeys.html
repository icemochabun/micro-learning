    1 min Verbal check · 1 min · Final recall

    Ongoing Habit formed

    He manages this himself now

    Mr Muthu connects hydration to his existing pill routine. His daughter stops reminding him. He mentions it to his friend at the AAC. The module took 8 minutes total — the habit is permanent.

📆 What arrives at Week 2 — Mr Muthu's reinforcement

A brief follow-up screen on the shared AAC tablet. Same topic, one new practical tip tied to his daily routine. He navigates it solo this time — no facilitation needed.

    Phase 1 — Admin

    Not a handbook — a 5-minute orientation

    A welcome email with a single link. Five-minute module: role overview, one policy, one safety note. Every item connected to a reason — "This waiver keeps the programme running for 100 more seniors." No 40-page PDF.

    5 min Info · 5 min · Template B
  2. 2
    Day 4 Email link
    Phase 2 — Culture

    Why this matters — an impact story

    A short impact story: Ahmad, 79, and his befriender Susan. Their Thursday calls started with one question. A 4-minute video. No quiz — just a reflection prompt. Jamie finishes it on his lunch break.

    5 min Scenario · 4 min · Template B
  3. 3
    Day 10 Email link
    Phase 3 — Task

    The hard scenario first

    A branching scenario: the first visit, a senior who doesn't want to talk. Three response options. Jamie makes a choice, sees the consequence, tries another path. He arrives at his first placement knowing what to do if it goes quiet.

    7 min Branching scenario · 7 min · Template B
  4. 4
    First placement day Notification

    A one-tap quick-reference card

    A push notification on the morning of his visit: "You're ready. Here's your quick-ref card." Three bullet points. Arrival → introduction → open question. He screenshots it.

    1 min Quick-ref · 1 min · Just-in-time
  5. ✓
    Month 1 onwards Retained & returning

    Jamie comes back — and tells others

    He completes his first placement and receives a "Week 1 Complete" badge. Monthly 2-minute micro-updates keep him connected between visits. He books a second placement. Total onboarding time: 18 minutes across 10 days.

🎓 What keeps volunteers coming back — monthly micro-updates

After onboarding, a 2-minute email arrives each month — a new scenario, a reflection prompt, or a programme update. No obligation, no login required. Just enough to stay connected and confident.
