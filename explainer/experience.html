Hook · 30 sec

Mr Tan, 82, was his usual cheerful self at breakfast. By lunch he doesn't recognise his daughter.

This is delirium — an acute, sudden change in awareness and cognition. It looks like many things. Most staff miss the early signs. You won't, after this module.

⏱ This module takes about 3 minutes. One idea. One skill. One quiz.

Core · 90 sec

Three signs that signal delirium

Delirium comes on fast — hours or days, not weeks. Look for changes from the person's baseline:

  1. 1
    Sudden confusion or disorientation — can't say where they are, what day it is, or who people are. Onset within hours.
  2. 2
    Fluctuating alertness — lucid one moment, drowsy or agitated the next. Worse in the afternoon or evening ("sundowning").
  3. 3
    Unusual behaviour — suddenly withdrawn, calling out, picking at clothes, refusing food when they normally eat well.
📋 Delirium is a medical emergency. It is reversible if caught early — infections, medication, dehydration are common causes.

Retrieval · 60 sec

Mrs Lim, 78, has been quieter than usual since morning. What should you do?

✓ Complete

You've got the skill. Now use it.

Any sudden change in behaviour — even quiet withdrawal — is a red flag. Report it immediately. Your observation is the first line of protection for your resident.

Your one action: Next time you hand over, explicitly mention if any resident seems different from their baseline — quieter, more confused, or more agitated than usual.

Hook · 30 sec · Seniors

💧

Staying hydrated keeps your energy up

Water helps your body work well. It keeps your joints moving and your mind sharp — even a little dehydration can make you feel tired or confused.

⏱ About 2 minutes. One tip. One question.

Core · 60 sec · Seniors

Aim for 6–8 glasses every day

You do not need to drink them all at once. Try one simple habit:

  1. 1
    Morning — one glass when you wake up, before breakfast.
  2. 2
    With meals — one glass with each meal counts. That is already 3.
  3. 3
    Your pills — take your morning pill with a full glass. That is your first glass done.
📋 You do not need to feel thirsty to be dehydrated. Older adults feel thirst less. Sip regularly.

Question · Seniors

Do you usually drink 6–8 glasses of water a day?

✅

✓ Well done!

You did it!

Keep a small water bottle near you during the day. Link one glass to something you already do — like your morning pill.

Core · 90 sec · Volunteers

Our mission & values

"Every visit you make changes someone's day."
  1. 1
    Dignity — every senior is an individual with a full life story. We listen first.
  2. 2
    Consistency — showing up regularly matters more than perfection. Your presence is the point.
  3. 3
    Safety — your wellbeing matters too. Escalate concerns; you are not alone.
