01 — What

What is micro-learning?

Not a shortened course. A fundamentally different approach to how people learn.

"Short, focused bursts of learning designed to meet specific knowledge outcomes — one objective, one module."

Emerson & Berge, adapted

Traditional training

  • Half-day or full-day sessions
  • Multiple objectives at once
  • Annual or quarterly schedule
  • 20–30% completion rate
  • Retention drops sharply within days

Micro-learning

  • 2–10 minutes per module
  • One objective per module
  • Spaced across Day 1 → 7 → 30
  • 80–90% completion rate
  • 75% recall at 31 days (vs 21%)

The forgetting curve

Without reinforcement, most learning is lost within 24 hours.

02 — How

How it works

Three cognitive mechanisms drive the results.

Spaced repetition

Re-presenting content at increasing intervals (Day 0 → 7 → 30) forces active recall at the exact moment memory starts to fade. Each recall strengthens the neural pathway.

Learn
Day 7
Reinforce
Day 30
Confirm

Chunking

Working memory holds ~7 items (Sweller's Cognitive Load Theory). One concept per module stays within that limit — learners can process fully without cognitive overload.

Active retrieval

Every module ends with a quiz or scenario — not passive review. Retrieving information from memory strengthens retention far more than re-reading the same content.

03 — Who

Designed for three audiences

Each audience needs a different design approach — same pedagogy, different expression.

Care Staff

AAC workers, deskless, high-stakes

Learning approach: Andragogy — intrinsic motivation, problem-centred, needs the "why" upfront

  • Clinical protocols (delirium, falls, PPE)
  • Soft skills via daily scenarios
  • Resilience moments (3 min, on-demand)
Mobile-first 44px tap targets 2–10 min
📱 Also via WhatsApp & SMS · 98% adoption in remote clinics

Senior Learners

AAC members, aged 60+

Learning approach: Geragogy — transcendent motivation, leverages lived experience, practical application

  • Health self-management
  • Digital literacy (step-by-step)
  • Social connection skills

Social service orgs, intrinsically motivated

🇸🇬 Singapore implementation context

AIC Learning Network — 360+ courses (92 free e-courseware) via CCLMS for community care sector staff across Singapore's AACs, nursing homes, and day rehabilitation centres.
Age Well SG (2024) — Refreshed Community Care Skills Standards Framework (CCSSF) specifically for AAC staff. Endorsed roadmap covering clinical, behavioural, and psychosocial skills — micro-learning maps directly onto these competencies.
The gap: CCLMS offers LMS-based courses, not true micro-learning. A mobile-first, 2–10 min module layer — delivered via WhatsApp or an app — would be a complementary, additive layer on top of existing AIC infrastructure.

Research evidence

AIC Learning Network
360+ courses (92 free e-courseware) for Singapore community care sector via CCLMS. Refreshed CCSSF for AAC staff in 2024 as part of Age Well SG — endorsed training roadmap covering clinical, behavioural and psychosocial skills.
JMIR Medical Education (2023) — Minnity Platform
26 older adult care staff across 3 organisations completed 2–3 min modules on dementia care and COVID hygiene. Result: high perceived knowledge retention, confidence in applying knowledge, and improved work performance.
Scientific Reports RCT (2025)
118 elderly participants. Microlearning video clips (intervention) vs standard care (control). Result: significant improvement in medication adherence and health literacy in the intervention group.
